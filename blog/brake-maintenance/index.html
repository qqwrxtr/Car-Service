At Domain, we've seen too many preventable brake emergencies. This guide will help you identify the warning signs before they become dangerous situations, potentially saving you thousands in repairs and, more importantly, keeping you safe on the road.

5 Critical Warning Signs That Demand Immediate Action

Safety First

If you notice any of these warning signs, have your brakes inspected immediately. Don't waitâ€”brake failure can happen without warning.

1. Unusual Sounds

  • Squealing: High-pitched noise when braking - wear indicators warning you
  • Grinding: Metal-on-metal contact - immediate service required
  • Clicking: Possible brake pad or hardware issues

DANGER LEVEL: HIGH if grinding

2. Pedal Feel Changes

  • Spongy pedal: Air in brake lines or fluid leak
  • Hard pedal: Brake booster or vacuum issue
  • Pedal to floor: Critical brake system failure

DANGER LEVEL: CRITICAL if pedal goes to floor

3. Vehicle Pulling

  • Pulls to one side: Uneven brake pad wear or stuck caliper
  • Steering wheel vibration: Warped brake rotors
  • Car drifts when braking: Brake imbalance

DANGER LEVEL: MEDIUM - affects vehicle control

4. Dashboard Warning Lights

  • Brake warning light: Low fluid or system malfunction
  • ABS light: Anti-lock brake system issue
  • Parking brake light: May indicate brake fluid level

DANGER LEVEL: HIGH - electronic system alert

What Causes These Problems?

Normal Wear

Brake pads typically last 25,000-70,000km depending on driving habits. City driving with frequent stops wears brakes faster than highway driving.

Heat Damage

Excessive heat from hard braking can warp rotors, boil brake fluid, and cause brake fade. Mountain driving and towing increase heat buildup.

Fluid Issues

Brake fluid absorbs moisture over time, reducing effectiveness. Low fluid levels indicate leaks that must be addressed immediately.

Monthly Brake Safety Check

5-Minute Safety Inspection

Visual Checks

  • Brake fluid level: Check reservoir - should be between MIN/MAX lines
  • Fluid color: Should be clear to light amber, not dark or contaminated
  • Visible leaks: Check under car for brake fluid puddles

Performance Tests

  • Pedal feel: Should be firm, not spongy or hard
  • Stopping distance: Note any changes in how quickly you stop
  • Noise check: Listen for any unusual sounds when braking
